# My Experiences Creating Conversational AI & Analyzing Data: Learning Typebot & Metabase

I have completed two very hands-on projects that have dramatically altered my view of how software is designed and analyzed. The first project was creating an interactive chatbot to support the Unmukt cultural festival through Typebot. The second project involved performing business analysis by utilizing Metabase. At first, these tools seemed to have two completely different uses with regard to their intended application, one is a medium for communicating in a creative way through chat while other allows for data and numerical analysis through charts and tables.As I worked on both projects, I had realized that there is an intersection between conversational design and data analytics. This post serves to share my personal insights throughout the process.

## Creating Conversations: Understanding Typebot

I wanted to offer freshers an opportunity to navigate through the noise of the festival while still receiving guidance from a senior, not just from the perspective of an intelligent, lifeless machine. To do this, I knew that my design needed to be extremely user-centric. I utilized fun and colourful emojis, non-formal language, and a very warm "senior to junior" tone to provide the user with a feeling of comfort.

The technical knowledge I gained from this project was mainly a result of dealing with complex logical situations. Using dynamic variables to provide the user with a decent chat (their name) and also using "jump blocks" made me have to think of logic in a different way. I ensured that all jumps worked properly, meaning that there could be no dead ends in the conversation; This required an exorbitant amount of testing and previewing. In total, I spent around 10 hours testing and previewing the bot over 200 times. I learned that the small nuances of our communication to users can significantly impact their overall experience with our chatbot.


## Transforming Data into Communicative Numbers through Learning Metabase

 The purpose of building a chatbot for the project was to communicate with users and retrieve their requested information; whereas Metabase is about understanding your data and communicating with your data. Raw business data typically looks like a big, ugly table of numbers that is difficult to read. With Metabase, I was able to learn how to get this data into a clean structured visual format, like a bar chart or pie chart and easily share that information (story) with my audience.

This allowed me to learn how to identify trends in my business, such as peak sales times, and which products are performing the best. My favourite function of Metabase was creating dashboards. By compiling multiple charts on one screen, I can now display complex business metrics for anyone to understand at a glance. I have learned that data is only useful if you can clearly display it in a visual manner.

## Connecting Both Tools in Reality

Through my creative process on these projects, I began to see the bigger picture of how modern applications function in reality. There is no separation between conversational AI and data analysis, they're part of one single entity and as such function as a cohesive unit. The chatbot collects the information from the user. Those data are stored in a database.

Then data has been collected and saved to a database, so that we are able to connect that database with Metabase so that we can see the results in graphical form. As an example of this, if hundreds of freshers were to use the event chatbot, then we could create a chart using Metabase to show what percentage of freshers who used the event chatbot would like to go to the food stalls instead of the main stage area of the event. This would provide event organizers with valuable data that would allow them to make informed decisions regarding future events and would enable event organizers to base their decisions on facts provided by data rather than assumptions made by opinion.The relationship between these two types of technology help me to learn and gave me experience from working on these projects.

 ## Future Prospects and Challenges

One of the hardest challenges faced in any project is that there will always be issues that arise, and I had quite a few with the Typebot project. One of my struggles was using the Instagram API to embed live videos into the chat, as they wouldn't load due to privacy settings. Instead of giving up on that idea, I changed things up by using high-quality images of the campus instead of the videos and provided a text description of a "Hidden Spot" on campus instead. Challenges I faced were the logic loops where the bot got stuck; luckily for me, I received some help from my friend, Madhu Meena while debugging those and there were no gaps left after the manual debugging and testing process occurred.

From these challenges I had forced myself to solve the problem as my own which may also help me to start my own startup. I can build chatbots to capture customer leads for a business and use Metabase to track how many sales I have and how fast I'm growing. These projects helped me see that technology has the greatest impact when a user creatively designs a product and that product is paired with good data analysis, so I'm looking forward to applying what I've learned from these projects to future technology projects.
